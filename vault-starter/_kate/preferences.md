# Kate Preferences

## Working Style
- Sunday night is the main triage window.
- Prefer bullet summaries over narrative paragraphs.
- Keep outputs concise and action-oriented.

## Tool Preferences
- Email outputs should be draft-first.
- Presentation style should be minimal and executive-ready.

## Triage Labels
Apply these labels consistently during inbox and calendar triage.

### Priority (mutually exclusive)
- `P0` — Exec escalation, customer deadline <48h, Tier 1 VIP
- `P1` — Customer meetings needing prep, action-required emails
- `P2` — Internal updates, enablement, non-urgent
- `P3` — Newsletters, distro noise, automated alerts

### Type (pick one)
- `customer` — External-facing engagement (BlueKC, Rogers, Stryker, Cencora, BD, etc.)
- `partner` — Partner-facing (Epic, Ontinue, McKinsey)
- `internal` — 1:1s, team syncs, v-team calls
- `enablement` — Tech talks, office hours, training, conferences
- `ops` — Capacity, pipeline hygiene, CRM follow-up, PAT renewals
- `social` — Team events, outings

### Signal (apply all that fit)
- `action-required` — Needs reply, decision, or follow-up from you
- `prep-needed` — Meeting requires pre-read or context assembly
- `fyi` — Informational only, no response needed
- `time-sensitive` — Expiring deadline (registration, token, scheduling window)
- `customer-facing` — External attendees or recipients present
