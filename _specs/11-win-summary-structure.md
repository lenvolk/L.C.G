## High-Signal Example Patterns

### Pattern A: Winning Wednesdays Recap Email

Common subject forms:

- `HLS Win Room | Winning Wednesdays - <date> Recap`
- `Win Room | Winning Wednesdays - Recap <date>`
- `Winning Weds - Win Room Recap - <date>`

Common sections in body:

- session focus / themes
- customer-deal callouts
- compete posture
- decisions and next steps

### Pattern B: Win Wire Announcement Email

Common subject forms:

- `WIN WIRE: <headline with value metric>`
- `Win Wire: <customer> <value> <motion>`
- `HLS Win Wire: <customer> <outcome>`

Common section flow:

- headline deal claim
- value metrics ($, seats, term)
- why Microsoft won
- leadership reinforcement
- reuse value for storytelling

### Pattern C: STU Teams Highlights

Common sources:

- Friday Huddle threads
- STU/CSU recurring sync threads
- STU update and pipeline facilitator notes

Common signal types:

- coordination asks
- milestone / pipeline movement
- blocker escalation
- recap notes for attendance / continuity

### Pattern D: Patty D Deal Summary Naming Drift

Observed:

- Exact phrase `Patty D Deal Summary` is rare/absent.
- Equivalent content often appears as:
  - `Deal Overview`
  - `Commercial Summary`
  - `MACC Summary`
  - `Win Wire`

Implication:

- Retrieval must include alias and phrase variants instead of exact-match only.

## Canonical Reusable Structure

Use this structure for all weekly or ad hoc summary outputs:

1. Headline
2. Executive Highlights
3. Scoreboard table
4. Risks and Escalations
5. Decisions and Asks
6. Owner Action Queue
7. Confidence and Gaps
8. Evidence Links

## Scoreboard Table Standard

Required columns:

- Customer
- Motion
- Metric
- Why It Matters
- Owner
- Source

## Quality Rules

- De-duplicate cross-posted signals.
- Keep bullets action-oriented and owner-explicit.
- Include confidence plus missing-data notes.
- Preserve source links for audit and trust.
